So WTF are roman numerals.

Roman numerals are a numeral system that originated in ancient Rome and remained the usual way of writing numbers throughout Europe well into the Late Middle Ages. Numbers are written with combinations of letters from the Latin alphabet, each letter with a fixed integer value. Modern style uses only these seven:

I V X L C D M
1 5 10 50 100 500 1K

Roman numerals of course were widely used for a shit ton of purposes, but one of the coolest in my opinion is the legion number, in the Roman legions, numerals were used to denote the legion number, the cohort number, and the century number. The Roman military structure was highly organized and hierarchical, and numerals played a crucial role in identifying and organizing different units.

  • Legion Number:

    • The Roman legions were numbered with Roman numerals, typically from I to XXX (1 to 30). Each legion had its own unique numeral designation, and this numeral was an essential part of the legion's identity. For example, Legio III Augusta represented the third legion, and Legio X Fretensis represented the tenth legion.

  • Cohort Number:

    • Legions were subdivided into cohorts, and each cohort was assigned a specific numeral. A legion typically had ten cohorts, and the cohort numerals ranged from I to X. For instance, in Legio III Augusta, the first cohort would be Cohors I, the second Cohors II, and so on.

  • Century Number:

    • The basic tactical unit within a Roman legion was the century, which consisted of approximately 80 to 100 men. Centuries were numbered within each cohort using Roman numerals. The first century of the first cohort, for example, would be known as Centuria I, while the second century of the second cohort would be Centuria II.
